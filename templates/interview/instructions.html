How Scoring Works

  • Each answer is scored 0–100% across four factors:
  • Coverage: relevant concepts/keywords (fuzzy matched, minor variations count).
  • Structure: clear definition first, then key points, example, and trade-offs.
  • Clarity & Conciseness: focused, within a reasonable length, avoids hedging.
  • Depth: specificity, correct terminology, and any numbers/metrics.
  • Weights vary by level (more emphasis on depth at higher levels).
  • Strong: ≥ 75% • Good: 50–74% • Needs Work: < 50%.
  • Your overall score is the average across questions; results include marks and a next-level recommendation.

Adaptive Mode

  • Starts at your current level ({{ current_level|default:"beginner" }}).
  • Next questions adjust based on your previous answer scores.
  • Target: {{ total_questions|default:9 }} questions (approx. 2 min each).
  • Keep answers focused; include definitions, trade-offs, and examples.

Answering Guidelines

  • Start with a definition, then key points, then a brief example.
  • Mention 3–5 relevant keywords (concepts, tools, APIs).
  • Quantify impact where possible (e.g., latency, memory, cost).
  • Keep it clear and concise; aim for a focused 60–180 words.
  • Avoid hedging like “maybe”, “I think”, “not sure”.

Marking & Results

  • Per-question score contributes to overall percentage.
  • Performance levels: Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced.
  • Strengths and weak areas summarized by keywords.
  • Skill Proficiency updates only after completing interviews.
